Georgia's process for creating her prints

The video displays some of Georgia's prints that she shared in the Grinnell exhibit "Green, Pink, and Yellow", a show featuring work by Georgia Carbone and Stella Lowery. Georgia's prints have a unique layered appearance that gives the effect of a collaged artwork. Georgia explained how she created this effect.

Georgia describes her process stating that she would roll ink on to a monotype plate. She would then cut thin shapes from sheets of acetate. Using the monotype plate, she would make a print onto the acetate, transferring the color on to the sheets. Finally, she used the acetate sheets and printed those on a piece of paper, resulting in the collaged appearance of her work.
